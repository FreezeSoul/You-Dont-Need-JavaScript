Celebrated Indian Authors

Meet the literary giants who have enriched Indian literature

Rabindranath Tagore

Poetry Fiction

Nobel laureate who reshaped Bengali literature and music. His profound works like Gitanjali continue to inspire generations worldwide.

R.K. Narayan

Fiction Humor

Creator of the beloved fictional town of Malgudi, known for his simple yet profound storytelling that captures the essence of Indian life.

Arundhati Roy

Literary Fiction Social Commentary

Booker Prize winner for "The God of Small Things," renowned for her lyrical prose and powerful exploration of social issues.

Vikram Seth

Fiction Poetry

Author of the epic "A Suitable Boy," celebrated for his meticulous detail and masterful portrayal of post-independence India.

Amitav Ghosh

Historical Fiction Adventure

Master storyteller of historical epics like "The Ibis Trilogy," blending rigorous research with compelling narratives.

Salman Rushdie

Magical Realism Literary Fiction

Internationally acclaimed for "Midnight's Children," known for his innovative narrative style and magical realism.

Chetan Bhagat

Contemporary Youth Fiction

Bestselling author who brought Indian English writing to mainstream audiences with relatable stories of modern India.

Ruskin Bond

Children's Literature Nature

Beloved children's author and nature enthusiast, capturing the beauty of the Himalayas and simple life with warmth.
